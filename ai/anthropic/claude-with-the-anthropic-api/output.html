# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training Athlete

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete aiming to fuel marathon training with carbohydrate loading while adhering to vegetarian and dairy-free requirements:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (110g), Fat (85g), Carbs (420g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with almond milk (240ml), banana (120g), chia seeds (15g), and maple syrup (20g).
  * Protein: 12g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 85g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Energy balls made with dates (80g), oats (30g), and almond butter (25g).
  * Protein: 8g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 55g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Chickpea and vegetable stir-fry with chickpeas (150g cooked), bell peppers (100g), broccoli (120g), and brown rice (120g cooked). Coconut oil for cooking (15g). Served with whole wheat bread (60g).
  * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Afternoon Snack (3:30 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with tahini (25g) and sliced apple (150g). Orange juice (250ml).
  * Protein: 8g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 75g

* **Dinner (6:30 PM):** Lentil and vegetable curry with red lentils (100g dry weight), sweet potato (200g), spinach (100g), onion (80g), garlic (10g), and coconut milk (240ml). Basmati rice (150g cooked).
  * Protein: 35g, Fat: 22g, Carbs: 95g

* **Evening Snack (8:30 PM):** Smoothie with frozen berries (100g), banana (100g), plant-based protein powder (25g), and oat milk (240ml).
  * Protein: 29g, Fat: 21g, Carbs: 45g

This meal plan emphasizes complex carbohydrates and plant-based proteins essential for marathon endurance, while remaining fully vegetarian and dairy-free. The high carbohydrate content (420g) supports glycogen loading for long-distance running performance.
